project summery

Developed a multiplayer, open-scenario VR medical simulation in Unreal Engine using C++, collaboratively with my team at ELDC, as part of an official office project. The simulation, centered around Tania, a 7-month-old infant, is designed to train healthcare professionals in recognizing and managing respiratory distress in infants. Learners are placed in a realistic emergency department environment where they must assess Tania’s symptoms—such as reduced feeding, noisy breathing, and chest retractions—and make timely clinical decisions. The module encourages critical thinking, teamwork, and effective communication in a dynamic, real-time scenario. It includes a post-session debriefing system that allows participants to review their actions, analyze mistakes, and reinforce correct responses for future practice. My contributions included developing multiplayer functionality, procedural event handling, and interactive VR elements to ensure a seamless, immersive learning experience. This project highlights the potential of VR-based medical training to improve clinical decision-making, teamwork, and emergency response skills in high-stakes pediatric care situations.
