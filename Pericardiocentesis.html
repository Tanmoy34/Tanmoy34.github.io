Developed an XR medical training simulation in Unreal Engine using C++, in collaboration with my team at MedVREducation, as part of an official office project approved by the American Heart Association (AHA) â€“ Pericardiocentesis: Emergency procedure for cardiac tamponade (n.d.). This project, titled Emergent Pericardiocentesis Simulator, is designed to train electrophysiologists and cardiology practitioners in performing this critical yet rare life-saving procedure. The immersive XR experience provides a realistic clinical environment where learners are guided through each procedural step, beginning with patient positioning, draping, and site preparation, followed by needle insertion, wire advancement, and dilator placement to expand the drainage site. Learners then use the pigtail catheter and stopcock to aspirate pericardial fluid, completing the procedure by confirming effusion resolution via intracardiac echocardiography (ICE). The simulation features an interactive performance assessment system, evaluating user efficiency, precision, and overall time to completion while providing instant feedback to enhance learning outcomes. My contributions included developing procedural logic, XR interaction mechanics, and the live performance feedback system to deliver a high-fidelity, data-driven simulation that promotes hands-on skill retention in a safe, virtual clinical environment.
