Developed a VR medical training simulation in Unreal Engine using C++, in collaboration with my team at MedVREducation, as part of an official office project focused on the Bougie Intubation procedure. This immersive training experience enables healthcare professionals to practice advanced airway management in a realistic, interactive environment. Learners begin with the laryngoscope in place, guide the angled tip of the Bougie through the vocal cords, and advance it until the characteristic bounce of the tracheal rings is felt. Working alongside a virtual assistant, they then insert the endotracheal tube, remove the instruments, inflate the balloon, and confirm placement through auscultation and end-tidal COâ‚‚ verification. The simulation provides all necessary affordances for procedural accuracy and realism while integrating an XR-based live scoring system that tracks learner performance in real time, assessing precision, timing, and adherence to protocol. My responsibilities included developing core procedural logic, instrument interaction systems, and the live performance scoring mechanism for XR integration. This project showcases the potential of VR and XR technologies in delivering high-fidelity, data-driven training that improves clinical skill acquisition and confidence in airway management.
