project summery

Developed a multiplayer, open-scenario VR medical simulation in Unreal Engine using C++, in collaboration with my team at ELDC, as part of an official office project designed to train healthcare professionals in managing pediatric seizure emergencies. The scenario centers on Ella Robinson, an eight-year-old child with a history of epilepsy who suffers a seizure and injury following a fall at school. Learners assume the role of emergency medical service (EMS) providers responding to the case, where they must assess the patient’s condition, administer medication, and treat related injuries. As the seizure persists despite medication, the learner coordinates with the air ambulance team for patient transport to a healthcare facility and may be required to perform intubation en route, based on the patient’s response. Set within a photorealistic and fully interactive virtual environment, the simulation incorporates all necessary affordances to enhance realism, teamwork, and procedural efficiency. My contributions included developing multiplayer systems, medical interaction logic, and real-time event sequencing to ensure an immersive, responsive training experience. This project demonstrates the use of VR technology in improving decision-making, teamwork, and emergency response skills in pediatric critical care scenarios.
