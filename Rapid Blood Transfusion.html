Developed a VR medical training simulation in Unreal Engine using C++, in collaboration with my team at MedVREducation, as part of an official office project focused on the Rapid Blood Transfusion procedure. Centered around Richard Mauk, a 52-year-old patient admitted for an urgent transfusion after vomiting blood, this simulation trains healthcare professionals to perform a rapid blood transfusion using the Belmont Rapid Infuser. The experience includes both training and assessment modes, enabling learners to practice independently and receive real-time performance feedback. Participants begin by confirming patient identity, checking allergies, and obtaining informed consent from the patientâ€™s relative before proceeding to equipment setup, including assembling and priming the Belmont Rapid Infuser. Learners must then verify blood products, program the infuser according to medical orders, and complete the transfusion while maintaining aseptic technique. Upon completion, they safely dispose of all biohazard materials following clinical standards. The simulation provides all necessary tools, interactions, and procedural affordances within a realistic virtual environment. My contributions included developing core procedural systems, interaction logic, and performance optimization, ensuring a seamless, accurate, and immersive learning experience for medical professionals.
