project summery

Developed a short-duration (5-minute) procedural VR training simulation in Unreal Engine using C++, in collaboration with my team at ELDC, as part of an official office project focused on Goniometry Assessment for physical therapy. Designed for Physical Therapists (PTs), PT Technicians, and PT Students, this simulation enables learners to practice measuring joint range of motion using a virtual Goniometer in a realistic, interactive environment. The scenario centers on Sydney Smith, a 30-year-old patient recovering from an accident and undergoing rehabilitation. Learners begin by confirming patient identity and performing hand hygiene, then proceed to measure the knee, hip, and ankle joints by accurately aligning the fulcrum, stationary arm, and moveable arm of the Goniometer. Upon completing the assessment, results are compared against reference values for normal range of motion, considering the patient’s age, activity level, and health status. The simulation features an integrated debriefing system and ChatGPT-powered feedback module, allowing learners to review their performance, identify errors, and receive real-time guidance for improvement. My contributions included developing core procedural logic, integrating AI-based debriefing, and optimizing interaction systems to deliver an efficient, educational, and highly immersive learning experience.
